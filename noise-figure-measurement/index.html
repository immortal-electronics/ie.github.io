Overview

The Amplifier’s contribution to the overall noise in a system is called as Noise figure. We can describe that even as the ratio between the signal-to-noise ratio at the input of an amplifier to the signal-to-noise ratio at the output of an amplifier

The above calculator was designed for you to calculate the noise figure of up to 10 cascaded amplifiers. To find the total noise figure and overall gain of cascaded amplifiers, just enter the no. of cascaded amplifiers along with noise and gain (in dB), then simply click “Calculate”.

Note

While we are designing a circuit, noise consideration is very important, that too if the circuit is dealing with the weak signals, the noise should be mandatorily considered. One more important point to remember is Noise cannot be eliminated completely from a circuit, but it is the electronic circuit designer’s role to make sure that the noise effect is minimum in the circuit so that it will not decrease the signal-to-noise ratio.

A system can get its best performance when the total noise figure is minimum. For an ideal amplifier, the signal-to-noise ratio at both input and output is infinite so the noise figure is 1, but this cannot be achieved by a real amplifier.
