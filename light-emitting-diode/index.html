Light Emitting Diode

The Light emitting diode is a two-lead semiconductor light source. In 1962, Nick Holonyak invented this light emitting diode. The LED is a special type of diode which has similar electrical characteristics like a PN junction diode. So the LED allows the flow of current in the forward direction and blocks the current in the reverse direction as same as a general purpose PN junction diode. The LED occupies the small area which is not as much as 1 mm2.

Working

As we already said the light emitting diode is similar to a PN junction diode, so when the led is forward biased, electrons are able to recombine with holes within the device, releasing energy in the form of photons. This impact is called electroluminescence, and the color of the light is determined by the energy band gap of the semiconductor.

From the above diagram, we can know the internal mechanism of the light emitting diode.

  1. From the diagram, we can observe that the n-type silicon is represented in red color and it contains the electrons, they are indicated by the black circles.

  2. The p-type silicon is in the blue color and it contains holes, they are indicated by the white circles.

  3. The power supply across the p-n junction makes the diode forward biased and pushing the electrons from n-type to p-type and pushing the holes from p-type to n-type.

  4. Electron and holes at the junction are combined.

  5. The photons are generated as the electrons and holes are recombined.

This mechanism inside the LED is expalined by Quantum theory, the theory expalins that when the electron comes down from the higher energy level to the lower energy level then, the energy emits from the diode in the form of a photon. The photon energy is equal to the energy gap between the two energy levels.

The flow of current in semiconductors is only caused when both electrons and holes move in the same and opposite directions of current respectively. So, due to the flow of these charge carriers, there will be a condition of recombination.

The recombination condition explains that here the electrons will move from conduction band to vacancy band of the semiconductor, this jump will make the electron to release some electromagnetic energy in the form of photons. The energy in the photons is equal to the forbidden energy gap.

The energy of a photon is represented in the mathematical form as the product of planks constant and electromagnetic radiation.
