Series and Parallel Circuits

A path between two or more nodes/junctions along which an electrical current can be carried is called as Circuit. These Circuits are classified into Series and Parallel circuits according to the arrangement of components in the circuit.

Before learning about series and parallel circuits we should learn about two terms, they are node and current flow.

  • A node is referred as an electrical junction between two or more components if we draw a schematic diagram of a circuit the nodes represent the wires between the components.

  • Current flow can be described as the rate at which the electrons move from positive terminal of the battery to negative terminal of the battery in the circuit.

Therefore these components are in said to be in series and this circuit is called a Series Circuit.

Equivalent Resistances in Series Circuits

There is a requirement to calculate the equivalent resistance in the circuit which has more than one resistor, this is because, as the number of resistors gets increased in a series circuit will decrease the current flow inside the circuit. For example, let us take a circuit with a voltage source of 5 V and a resistor of 10 Ω, then the current flowing in the circuit will be 0.5 A.

Now we will add one more resistor of the same value to the circuit in series now the current flowing in the circuit will be 0.25 A, this is because now the total resistance in the circuit is 10+10 = 20 Ω. So from this, we can say that in the series circuit if the resistance is doubled the current flow will be halved.

Parallel Circuits

We can say that a circuit is a parallel circuit when a component in the circuit shares two common nodes, and if a different amount of current flow through each component of the circuit. For example, see the below circuit with three resistors and a battery connected in parallel.

So from this, we can say that the current is getting divided among the components of the circuit, in this condition we can say that the circuit is a parallel circuit.

Note: In Series Circuits, Voltage will be divided and in Parallel Circuits, Current will be divided.
