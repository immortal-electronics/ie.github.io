Temperature Coefficient of Resistance

Temperature is one of the most influencing factors of resistance, the relation between temperature and resistance has been a crucial part in many electronic circuits. In some circuits, the resistance may increase with temperature and sometimes this may decrease. So, it this temperature coefficient of resistance is one of the fundamental things to learn.

The resistance of a material is based on different external factors acting on the material, One of these is the number of collisions that occur between the charge carriers and atoms in the material. Here the number of collisions will be increased as the temperature is increased, therefore we can state that there will be a marginal increase in the resistance of material as the temperature is increased.

There is also an exception for the above condition in some cases, this is due to the negative temperature coefficient of resistance available for that material. This negative temperature coefficient of resistance condition can be explained as a case where with the increase of temperature further charge carriers are released which will result in a decrease in resistance with temperature. This condition is mainly observed in semiconductor materials.

While we observe the relation between resistance and temperature, in normal room conditions the temperature coefficient follows a linear path, this is limited by thermal vibrations of the atoms. If the temperatures are below normal conditions, i.e. less than 15 K, then the resistivity will be controlled by different factors like impurities and available carriers.
