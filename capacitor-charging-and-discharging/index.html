Capacitor Charging and Discharging

20 Jun 2018 ©Immortal Electronics.

Aim

To determine the charging and disharging capabilites of a Capacitor and to calculate its time constant.

Apparatus

  1. 6V Battery (Quantity - 1)
  2. Large Capacitor (>1000 uF) (Quantity - 2)
  3. 1 kΩ Resistor (Quantity - 2)
  4. Switch

Note

  • The Large Valued Capacitors are used in this experiment to produce a time constant which is slow enough to track with the help of a voltmeter and a stopwatch.

Cross Reference

For any doubts regarding Capacitors, refer Text Book - Capacitors

For any doubts regarding RC and L/R Time Constants, refer Text Book - RC and L/R Time Constants

Objectives

  1. Learning about capacitor’s charging and discharging actions
  2. How to calculate time constant

Procedure

  1. Connect the circuit in the form of Charging Circuit
  2. Now measure the volatge across the capacitor when the switch is closed
  3. You can observe the readings of voltage will increase slowly, rather than a sudden pace.
  4. You can reset the volatge across the capacitor to Zero by shorting the terminals of capacitor with a piece of wire
  5. The Time Constant(T), can be calculated by multiplying the resistor value in the circuit with the circuit capacitance.
  6. For Example: If we have a 1 kΩ resistor in circuit and a 1000 uF Capacitor, then the time constant is 1 Second.
  7. We can describe this time as the amount of time it takes for the capacitor voltage to increase approximately 63.2% from its present value to its final value, i.e the voltage of the battery.
  8. If we plot a graph for Charging Capacitor and time, we can see a inverse exponential graph.
  9. The Time Constant value can be changed by modifying the resistor and capacitor values in the circuit.
  10. Now connect the circuit in the form of Discharing Circuit
  11. Repeat the experiment simillar to charging circuit, but this time the voltage reaches the full battery voltage intially when switch is closed and gradually decreases when switch is opened.
  12. Calculate the Time Constant for this circuit even.

Precautions

  1. The Large Valued Capacitors are mostly electrolytic type, i.e these capacitors are polarity senstive, so be careful while connecting the terminals. If they are interchanged by mistake it may lead to explosion of the capacitor even at a low voltage of 6V, the exploded capacitors will release caustic chemicals and foul smell.
