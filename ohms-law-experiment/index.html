Ohm's Law Experiment

13 Jun 2018 ©Immortal Electronics.

Aim

To calculate the Voltage, Current and Resistance Values using Ohm’s Law.

Apparatus

  1. 9V Battery
  2. Digital Multi Meter
  3. A Resistor (Suggested : >10 KΩ and <100 KΩ)
  4. Pen and Paper

Cross Reference

For any doubts regarding Ohm’s Law, refer Text Book - Ohm’s Law

Objectives

  1. Usage of Ohm’s Law
  2. Usage of Digital Multi Meter

Illustration

Procedure

  1. Take a Resistor and note down its value for future use. (If you don’t know its value then use multimeter to find the resistance of the resistor)
  2. Build a circuit with a battery and the resistor as shown in the above schematic diagram
  3. Now, set the multimeter to DC Voltage range and measure the voltage across the resistor, as shown in the illustration
  4. Note down the voltage value.
  5. Now, set the multimeter to highest current range available.
  6. To connect the multimeter, now, just break the circuit and connect the multimeter in series with battery and resistor.
  7. Vary the current ranges in multimeter until you get a reading without many fluctuations.
  8. Note down the current value.
  9. Now, using Ohm’s law and by using Voltage and Resistance reading calculate current value, the calculated current value should be almost equal to the reading given by the multimeter.
  10. Similarly, using Ohm’s Law and any other two values calculate the remaining value, the calculated value should be almost equal to its multimeter reading.
  11. You can also now change the resistance and do the experiment again, but in any case, the voltage value will not change because we are not changing the battery in the experiment.

Precautions

  1. If you are measuring resistance, make sure you do not touch your bare hands to multimeter probes, because body-to-body resistance may affect the original resistance value.
