555 Audio Oscillator

16 Jun 2018 ©Immortal Electronics.

Aim

To learn about the Astable multivibrator or Oscillator using 555 Timer.

Apparatus

  1. 12V Battery or two 6V Battery (According to availability)
  2. NE 555 Timer
  3. LEDs (Quantity -2)
  4. 1 MΩ Resistor (Quantity -1)
  5. 100 KΩ Resistor (Quantity -1)
  6. 510 Ω Resistors (Quantity -2)
  7. 0.1 µF Capacitor, Non-polarized (Quantity -1)
  8. Audio Detector or Oscillator

Cross Reference

For any doubts regarding Multivibrators, refer Text Book - Multivibrators

Objectives

  1. Usage of 555 Timer as Astable Multivibrator
  2. Learning about the Duty Cycle

Procedure

  1. Connect the circuit as shown above with all resistors and capacitor, then the 555 Timer will produce oscillating signals.
  2. The LEDs will turn On and OFF with the help of Square wave output voltage.
  3. This circuit works on the principle of alternately charging and discharging a capacitor.
  4. The discharging of capacitor is done when the capacitor is connected to the “Discharge” terminal of 555 timer.
  5. The capacitor will be discharged when the “Threshold voltage” of 555 Timer increases than the 2/3rd value of Vcc.
  6. The capacitor will be charged when the “Trigger voltage” of 555 Timer crosses the 1/3rd value of Vcc.
  7. Now connect the Threshold and Trigger pins of 555 Timer to the capacitor so that we can get an output of Saw-Tooth wave form which alters between 1/3rd and 2/3rd values of Supply Voltage.
  8. Note down the current value.
  9. The Capacitor will receive its charging current from the series combination of 1 MΩ and 100 kΩ resistors, but the discharging current has to pass only through a 100 kΩ resistor.
  10. This results a condition where charging time is greater than the discharging time.
  11. The Output terminal of the 555 Timer will produce a Square-wave signal which will be “high” when capacitor is charging and it will be “low” when capacitor is discharing.
  12. We can observe this with the help of two LEDs in the circuit, the LEDs will be turning ON and OFF alternatively, that too with a variation of time which is due to the difference between charging and discharing time of the capacitor.
  13. The inequalities between the high and low times of the square wave is referred to be the Duty Cycle of the wave.
  14. A 50% Duty Cycle means, equal amount of high and low times of the wave. For example if the high time is 20% of total cycle and low time is 80% of total cycle, then it is referred as 20% Duty Cycle.
  15. Use an Audio detector or an Oscilloscope to observe the wave forms developed by this circuit. Change the resistor and capacitor values and observe the difference.

Precautions

  1. Build the circuit with atmost care, don’t make any mistakes which will return you the errors finally.
